What This Shoot Is About

Core Brief

This shoot communicates the platform story — what makes Man Matters different from any other hair solution. It is not a product walkthrough. It is not ingredient-focused. The focus is on what MM does as a platform: doctor consultation, personalised treatment, stage-based assessment, ongoing guidance. The ad can include product in editing but the script and on-screen communication must be platform-first.

Content Strategy — Platform-Focused Communication

What "Platform-Focused" Means

Not this"Minoxidil + Procapil + Biotin Gummies — yeh sab hai Man Matters kit mein." (product-first framing)
This"Man Matters pe doctor tumhara exact stage identify karta hai — phir tumhare liye specific plan banata hai. No guesswork." (platform-first framing)

Platform Differentiators to Script Around

1
Doctor-led diagnosis

Not self-diagnosis. Not random internet advice. A doctor reviews your hair stage and prescribes your plan. This is a clinical platform, not a product store.

2
Stage-based personalisation

MM identifies where you are in your hair loss journey before recommending anything. Stage 1, 2, 3 — each has a different plan. You get what's right for your stage, not a generic kit.

3
Ongoing guidance, not one-time purchase

App check-ins, doctor follow-up, routine tracking. MM stays with you through the journey — not just at the point of sale.

4
CTA — platform action

"Man Matters app pe apna hair stage check karo — doctor se consult lo." Drives app download and assessment, not direct product purchase.

Guardrails

Safe territory

  • Doctor consultation and guidance
  • Stage-based assessment (app)
  • Personalised treatment plan
  • Ongoing tracking and check-ins
  • Platform reliability and clinical backing
  • Product can appear in editing — not as the hero

Avoid

  • Leading with product name or ingredients
  • Guaranteed outcome claims
  • Stage-specific product promotion as the main message
  • Exact timelines ("results in 3 months")

Human review required before this content is used externally — medical/regulatory team must sign off on final scripts.
